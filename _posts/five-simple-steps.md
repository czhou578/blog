I have lived most of my life in America, and having encountered many trials and tribulations, I want to recommend some tips for fixing the country and ensuring the 21st century is the American age.

---

1. Not all problems are unprecedented. 

One of the biggest issues in America is that we treat every problem like an unprecedented problem. I've already explained it before in my very first post [here](https://czhou578.github.io/blog/2026/03/20/america's-biggest-problem.html). The thesis is that believing every problem is unprecedented leads to a scramble for cheap & easy solutions, since we believe a situation has no precedent. 

Consequently, cheap and easy solutions are often placed in top priority, ignoring the underlying issue, and never truly solving the real problems.

---

2. Produce more then you consume.

If you grow only 5 apples, then of course you won't be able to eat 6. If you only have 50k in your checkings account, you can't buy a 100k house. It's such a simple concept. I know its easy to ask for more then you deserve, but if everyone did that, we won't have a society anymore. 

Everybody's goal should be to create more value for society (and make the money as a side effect) rather then always clamoring for handouts from politicians. When subsidies are given out to make something cheaper, it's magic because you think its free, but actually you are paying once for the sticker price, and once again for the government to afford to subsidize it.
